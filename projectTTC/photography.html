Lighting

Lighting is going to be just as important as angles, if you can get a good understanding of the two of these, your pictures will come out better than most peoples.

Time of day and weather are going to play a very big part in how your pictures turn out. Shooting mid day in direct sun light is actually one of the worst things you can do, so try and plan for sunset or sun rise, or my personal favorite, a very cloudy day. If you have to shoot in the middle of the day, try and find shadier spots, this will help glares and washed out colors.

Another this that helps a lot with lighting is lens filters, the biggest one being a polarizer. This will help with reflections and glares as well.

Angles

One of the biggest things that I have learned is that getting to know angles is crutial. Its the first thing you can figure out and have planned before even getting the camera out. These are a lot of the go to shots I like to take.

  • 45 Degree - This one is probably the most universal to any car
  • Straight on front or rear
  • Details - Such as headlights, wheels, decals, etc.

Environment

Picking an environment that suits your vehichle is another great way to elevate your photography.

Some great starting points are:

  • Roads
  • Parking lots
  • Gas Stations

A great picture has taken all of these elements into consideration, one of these being off can bring a good picture down.
