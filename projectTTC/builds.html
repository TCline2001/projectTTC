Drift

Drift builds are focused on being able to control the car as it slides or "drifts".

Some aspects of a drift build would be:

  • Steering Angle Kits and Suspension
  • Wheels and Tires
  • Weight Reduction
  • Power Adders

Track

Track builds are focused on being able take the car around the track as fast as possible.

Some aspects of a track build would be:

  • Aero and Suspension
  • Wheels and Tires
  • Weight Reduction
  • Power Adders

Stance

Stance builds are focused purely on aesthetics and being low.

Some aspects of a stance build would be:

  • Suspension such as Bags or Coilovers
  • Wheels and Tires
  • Possibly body kits

Street

Street builds are a mix of all of the other builds meant to be driven on the street, not built as an extreme in any direction.

Some aspects of a street build would be:

  • Suspension
  • Wheels and Tires
  • Possible Body Kits
  • Power Adders

Classes of Cars

Cars typically fall into 3 classes, at least here in the US

Domestic
These are cars built in America, mostly your Chevys, Fords, and Dodges.
Import
These are cars that aren't made in America, like your Nissans, Porsches, Toyotas, etc.
Super and Hyper
These are very high end cars normally sitting above the $100,000 range, these are your Lamborghinis, Ferraris, Koenigseggs, etc.
